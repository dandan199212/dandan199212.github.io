What are the wants and needs of people in different parts of the world?

 

"Rule of thumb for the west world: the things we need the most are the things we desire the least."

Psychologist Abraham Maslow proposed the hierarchy of needs theory in his 1943 paper
"A Theory of Human Motivation". The framework remains influential in many fielfs till
today, including sociology research and management traning. It is characterized by a
pyramid with the basic needs at the bottom, and the need for self-actualization at the top.

------The Decision Book: 50 Models for Strategic Thinking

 

Is it true that we desire less of what we need and more of what we want as we become more well-off ?
A correlation analysis of 33 countries' GDP per capita and what people believe are the most important things in life shows:

  • GDP per Capita is associated more strongly with having a fulfilling job, good health, money for old age, free time, and internet access.
  • Internet access is the new "air" in the mordern world and considered indispensable.
  • As cell phones have become ubiquitous, people don't think about it anymore.
  • Being wealthier doesn't necessarily make people more altruistic, as there is not a strong correlation with GDP and helping others, or even children's education.
  • People are still more concernd with their own needs.
